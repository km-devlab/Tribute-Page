The Divine Son of Shiva

Lord Muruga

Lord Muruga, also known as Karthikeya, Skanda, and Subrahmanya, is one of the most beloved deities in Hinduism. He is the God of War, Wisdom, and Victory — worshipped with deep devotion across Tamil Nadu and the world.

Explore His Story

About Lord Muruga

Origins & Birth Story

Lord Muruga was born from the six sparks of fire that emerged from the third eye of Lord Shiva. These six sparks became six babies, each cared for by the six Krittika stars. Goddess Parvati embraced all six and they merged into one child with six faces — giving him the name Shanmukha, meaning "six-faced one."

Names & Significance

Lord Muruga is known by many sacred names — Karthikeya, raised by the Krittikas; Skanda, the one who leapt; Subrahmanya, dear to the Brahmanas; and Vel Murugan, wielder of the divine spear. Each name reflects a different dimension of his divine glory and the devotion of his followers across generations.

Divine Attributes

🔱

Vel (Spear)

The divine spear gifted by Goddess Parvati. It represents wisdom piercing through ignorance and is the most sacred symbol of Lord Muruga.

🦚

Peacock (Vahana)

The peacock is his divine vehicle, symbolizing grace, beauty, and the destruction of ego. It tramples the snake of desire beneath its feet.

🐓

Rooster Flag

The rooster on his flag symbolizes the dawn of wisdom and victory over darkness, ego, and spiritual ignorance.

👑

Six Faces (Shanmukha)

His six faces represent mastery over the six directions and the six qualities — knowledge, wisdom, peace, truth, patience, and grace.

🌺

Red Colour

Red is the sacred colour of Muruga, representing energy, power, divine fire, and the burning devotion of his followers.

⭐

Krittika Stars

The six Krittika stars are his divine mothers who nurtured him. They represent the nurturing energy of the cosmos.

Arupadai Veedu

The Six Sacred Abodes of Lord Muruga

01

Thiruchendur

📍 Thoothukudi, Tamil Nadu

Thiruchendur is a coastal temple standing majestically by the sea. It is here that Lord Muruga defeated the demon Surapadman and is worshipped as Senthilnatha. The sound of the waves and the temple bells together create an atmosphere of divine bliss unlike any other.

02

Thiruparankundram

📍 Madurai, Tamil Nadu

One of the oldest rock-cut temples in Tamil Nadu, Thiruparankundram is where Lord Muruga married Devasena, the daughter of Lord Indra, after his victory in war. The temple is carved directly into a hillside and is a breathtaking example of ancient Dravidian architecture.

03

Palani

📍 Dindigul, Tamil Nadu

Perched atop a hill, Palani is one of the most visited shrines in India. Lord Muruga is worshipped here as Dandayudhapani — the ascetic holding a staff — after renouncing worldly pleasures when Sage Narada awarded the fruit of wisdom to Lord Ganesha.

04

Swamimalai

📍 Kumbakonam, Tamil Nadu

At Swamimalai, Lord Muruga is revered as Swaminatha — the teacher who taught the meaning of the Pranava mantra Om to his own father Lord Shiva. This unique role of son becoming the guru of his father makes this abode especially significant.

05

Thiruthani

📍 Tiruvallur, Tamil Nadu

After his victory over Surapadman, Lord Muruga came to Thiruttani to cool his divine energy. Here he is worshipped as Subramanya. It is also the sacred place where he married Valli, the daughter of a tribal chieftain, symbolising his bond with all of humanity.

06

Pazhamudircholai

📍 Madurai, Tamil Nadu

Nestled amidst lush green hills and forests near Madurai, Pazhamudircholai is the only abode that is not on a hilltop but within a forest grove. Lord Muruga is worshipped here as Kumaraswamy surrounded by the beauty of nature in full bloom.
